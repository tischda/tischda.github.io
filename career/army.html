1994 - 1995 : French Army (F.F.S.A.)

French Army

After the defeat in 1945, Germany was divided in four zones. The Forces Françaises en Allemagne (FFA) were created to militarise their occupation zone. Heavy armored units were deployed to face the soviet threat. At the peak of the Cold War, the French Army counted up to 80.000 soldiers.

In 1990 after the fall of the Berlin wall, rumours started about its dissolution. Between 1991 and 1994, the FFA was reorganised, renamed to Forces Françaises Stationnées en Allemagne (FFSA) and eventually reduced to 25.000 men.

During my military service, I was first trained in a transmission regiment in Achern and then transferred to the headquarters in Baden-Baden. There, between rare field assignments, I was responsible for the conception and realization of a computerized management system for heating energies (budget analysis).

 

During this period, I became an expert in Microsoft Access and realised my first complete development project, from requirements gathering to user training.

         

In 2006, following the reorganisation of the French army and the removal of the conscription, there remains only 5000 soldiers in the FFSA.
