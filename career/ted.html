Euroscript - TED ALERT

01/2000 - 04/2000, infeurope S.A. Luxembourg
http://www.tenders.com/

Project lead (3 developers) for the technical analysis and modelling of the TED ALERT pilot project. Software architecture planning, sizing and performance analysis for data volumes exceeding 80 gigabytes per year. Evaluation of Intershop Enfinity as gateway for paying services such as translation and advertising.

The customer was a company of the Holtzbrinck group specialized in translation services : Euroscript. At the time, infeurope was owned partly by the Saarbrucker Zeitung who is running TED and is also part of the Holtzbrinck Group. But due to lack of resources and political divergences, the project was abandoned.

Nevertheless, it appears that Primesphere carried out most of the functionalities of this project (see link on top).

Background

TED (Tenders Electronic Daily) is the online version of the Supplement S of the Official Journal, produced by the Office for Official Publications of the European Communities. TED provides information on public contracts for works, supplies and services from all Member States of the European Union. Invitations to tender are published in all the 11 official EU languages.

The TED Alert Service is based on an automatic offline distribution service by fax or e-mail of public tenders coming from TED. The mission statement is becoming and being perceived as the Nr. 1 provider of information in the procurement (Public and Corporate) market place.

Functional overview

Original notices (about 600 daily) will be imported and indexed with their summary translations in the 11 EU languages.

The Web service will provide daily alerts, newsletters and a business and competition watch database. Users can set up custom search profiles based on CPV codes. On the other side, advertiser action profiles will match those criteria, therefore reaching the right users.

In addition, high quality translation and human help support services are available.
