Swiss Official Gazette of Commerce - SOGC

05/2000 - 09/2001, infeurope S.A. Luxembourg, BIT Bern
http://www.shab.ch/

Data modelling and implementation of the middle tier of the publication system. Oracle database installation, configuration and management on a multiprocessor Linux server. Performance tuning of the XML entity persistence (streaming compression with LOBS). Implementation of a secure replication mechanism between the online web database and the production database.

Background

The SOGC is the only official publication of the Swiss Confederation, which is published five times a week and contains the most recent official information and legally prescribed announcements. SOGC provides economic subjects – primarily companies in the segments of services, commerce, trade and industry – with relevant information on their business environment. The information provided is thus frequently of an operational character. 1200 – 1500 individual publications are published daily, of which the majority are legally binding. Today's SOGC is well positioned in the market due to the official information and, with a daily circulation of 60000 users, has excellent media values.

Most of the published SHAB notices (e.g. companies’ register, bankruptcies, calls for debts, procurement) are suitable for a structured data collection by means of forms and for an automated typesetting.

Functional overview

The project consists of two parts: SHAB Online and SHAB PDB.

SHAB ONLINE

  • Notification service for the notice suppliers. It provides forms for the authoring and submission of notices that shall be published in the SOGC (SHAB) as well as an environment to manage the generated web documents (target group: 1500 users)
  • Online publication of the SOGC (SHAB): The notices of all SHAB issues published within the previous year can be consulted by using an Online Search. It is also possible to download these issues in PDF format (target group: 60 000 users)
  • Online subscription: Daily e-mail-supported delivery of those notices of the current SHAB issue which correspond to the search profile that has been defined by the subscriber
  • General web presentation

SHAB PDB

  • Complete pre-press production
  • Publication database which stores all notices in XML-format. Attachments to the notices (PDF-files, Word-Documents, e.g.) are stored as well
  • Separate import interfaces (XML/SGML) for the notices of the Confederate Trade Register (EHRA) and the Institute for Intellectual Property (IGE)
  • Publication system including a workflow for the submitted notices as well as for the single SHAB issues.
  • Automated typesetting and layout in the Desktop Publishing System FrameMaker including automated vertical justification, colour-separated PDF generation for the print house, generation of publication information (issue, page, height, width etc. of a notice) for each notice that will be stored in the database
  • Web download interface (printable PDF files) for the print house
  • Billing system
