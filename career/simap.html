European Commission - SIMAP

03/1998 - 12/1999, infeurope S.A. Luxembourg, Brussels (Belgium), Murcia (Spain)
http://simap.eu.int/

Design and full implementation of the Système d'Information pour les Marchés Publics (SIMAP) under the direction of the European Commission. This project was very large and we used a broad range of tools to achieve the impossible, in a context of legal and political tension. For my first Internet project, it was one of the most rewarding as I learned a lot from the mistakes that have been made.

Background

SIMAP is an initiative of the European Commission to greatly increase the efficiency of public purchasing, through the use of information and communication technologies. It was launched in 1994, together with 15 Member States as well as some of the EU's European trading partners.

At the February 1995 summit on the Information Society, the leaders of the G7 nations decided that electronic tendering should be developed as a priority application for the new information superhighways. In a few years the whole procurement process could take place electronically, from internal planning through electronic tendering to invoicing and payment, offering enormous savings in time and increasing efficiency.

           

Functional overview

The development was meant to replace two existing pilot projects: a PC-based software with a graphical user interface to control data entry of standard public procurement forms, and a search and retrieval tool to disseminate the procurement information.

The Commission then decided to make SIMAP available generally through Internet, redesigning the forms to be used with the latest technology available. Again, the project divides into two parts :

  • Notification: The Purchaser Point allows purchasers (awarding authorities) to enter their procurement notices for publication in the Official Journal Supplement using online forms.
  • Dissemination: You can search through the Internet Procurement Database for general information on public procurement collected from selected Public Procurement sites, through the Procurement Opportunities Database for information on specific procurement opportunities currently available, and through the Purchaser Profiles to see what different organizations are buying, plan to buy or have bought in the past.

Technical overview

The two parts of the project have been developed separately.

  1. Notification:
    • Form based workflow implemented with Java servlets and a custom designed MVC pattern for dealing with multilingual (Unicode - UTF8) data;
    • A complex mapping required a separate MS Access database to capture the specification and generate more than 400 templates;
    • Extensive use of Javascript to provide cross-browser dynamic HTML;
    • Authentication is made through an LDAP Database that synchronizes user profiles with the main Oracle database;
    • After processing, the data is exported to the SEI (Système Editorial Informatisé) in SGML. Later, an XML gateway is added.
  2. Dissemination: Java applets and Perl CGI scripts retrieve information on procurement opportunities through a multi criteria search engine.

Besides this, we developed a wealth of utilities, such as a translation tool in PHP/MySQL, a statistical module and many scripts.
